% Matthew 1
\section{第一章}

% The Genealogy of Jesus

\subsection*{耶稣的家谱}

% This is the record of the genealogy of Jesus Christ, the son of David, the son of Abraham:
\N 这是大卫的子嗣、亚伯拉罕的子嗣耶稣基督的家谱记录：

% Abraham was the father of Isaac, Isaac the father of Jacob, and Jacob the father of Judah and his brothers.
\N 亚伯拉罕是以撒的父亲，以撒是雅各的父亲，而雅各是犹大和他兄弟们的父亲。

% Judah was the father of Perez and Zerah by Tamar, Perez the father of Hezron, and Hezron the father of Ram.
\N 犹大是由他玛生的法勒斯和谢拉的父亲，法勒斯是希斯仑的父亲，而希斯仑是兰的父亲。
    \footnote{希腊语亚兰，是兰的一个变体；在第4节也出现；参见历代志上2:9–10。}

% Ram was the father of Amminadab, Amminadab the father of Nahshon, and Nahshon the father of Salmon.
\N 兰是亚米拿达的父亲，亚米拿达是拿顺的父亲，而拿顺是撒门的父亲。

% Salmon was the father of Boaz by Rahab, Boaz the father of Obed by Ruth, Obed the father of Jesse,
\N 撒门是由喇合生的波阿斯的父亲，波阿斯是由路得生的俄备得的父亲，俄备得是耶西的父亲，
% and Jesse the father of David the king.
\N 而耶西是大卫王的父亲。

% Next:
接下来：

% David was the father of Solomon by Uriah’s wife,
大卫是由乌利亚的妻子生的所罗门的父亲，
% Solomon the father of Rehoboam, Rehoboam the father of Abijah, and Abijah the father of Asa.
\N 所罗门是罗波安的父亲，罗波安是亚比雅的父亲，而亚比雅是亚撒的父亲。
    \footnote{希腊语Asaph，是亚撒的一个变体；在第8节也出现；参见历代志上3:10。}

% Asa was the father of Jehoshaphat, Jehoshaphat the father of Joram, and Joram the father of Uzziah.
\N 亚撒是约沙法的父亲，约沙法是约兰的父亲，而约兰是乌西亚的父亲。

% Uzziah was the father of Jotham, Jotham the father of Ahaz, and Ahaz the father of Hezekiah.
\N 乌西亚是约坦的父亲，约坦是亚哈斯的父亲，而亚哈斯是希西家的父亲。

% Hezekiah was the father of Manasseh, Manasseh the father of Amon, Amon the father of Josiah,
\N 希西家是玛拿西的父亲，玛拿西是亚们的父亲，
    \footnote{希腊语阿摩司，是亚们的一个变体；在本节中出现两次；参见历代志上3:14。}
    亚们是约西亚的父亲，
% and Josiah the father of Jeconiah and his brothers at the time of the exile to Babylon.
\N 而约西亚是耶哥尼雅和他兄弟们的父亲，在那时被流放到巴比伦。

% After the exile to Babylon:
\N 在流放到巴比伦之后：

% Jeconiah was the father of Shealtiel, Shealtiel the father of Zerubbabel,
耶哥尼雅是撒拉铁的父亲，撒拉铁是所罗巴伯的父亲，
% Zerubbabel the father of Abiud, Abiud the father of Eliakim, and Eliakim the father of Azor.
\N 所罗巴伯是亚比玉的父亲，亚比玉是以利亚敬的父亲，而以利亚敬是亚所的父亲。

% Azor was the father of Zadok, Zadok the father of Achim, and Achim the father of Eliud.
\N 亚所是撒督的父亲，撒督是亚金的父亲，而亚金是以律的父亲。

% Eliud was the father of Eleazar, Eleazar the father of Matthan, Matthan the father of Jacob,
\N 以律是以利亚撒的父亲，以利亚撒是马但的父亲，马但是雅各的父亲，
% and Jacob the father of Joseph, the husband of Mary, of whom was born Jesus, who is called Christ.
\N 而雅各是约瑟的父亲，约瑟是马利亚的丈夫，而耶稣是玛丽亚生的，祂被称为基督。

% In all, then, there were fourteen generations from Abraham to David, fourteen from David to the exile to Babylon, and fourteen from the exile to the Christ.
\N 于是，总共从亚伯拉罕到大卫有十四代，从大卫到流放到巴比伦有十四代，从流放到基督也有十四代。

% The Birth of Jesus
\subsection*{耶稣诞生}

% This is how the birth of Jesus Christ came about: His mother Mary was pledged in marriage to Joseph, but before they came together, she was found to be with child through the Holy Spirit.
\N 这是耶稣基督诞生如何产生的：祂的母亲玛利亚被许诺与约瑟结婚，但在他们成婚之前，她被发现藉着圣灵有了身孕。
% Because Joseph her husband was a righteous man and was unwilling to disgrace her publicly, he resolved to divorce her quietly.
\N 因为她的丈夫约瑟是一个义人而不愿意公开使她受辱，他决心悄悄地与她离婚。

% But after he had pondered these things, an angel of the Lord appeared to him in a dream and said, “Joseph, son of David, do not be afraid to embrace Mary as your wife, for the One conceived in her is from the Holy Spirit.
\N 但在他思索完这些事情之后，主的一位使者在梦中向他显现并说，“约瑟，大卫的子嗣，不要害怕迎娶玛丽亚作你的妻子，因为在她里面受孕的那一位是源自圣灵的。
% She will give birth to a Son, and you are to give Him the name Jesus, because He will save His people from their sins.”
\N 她会生一个儿子，而你要将名字耶稣给祂，因为祂会将祂的子民从他们的罪恶中拯救。”

% All this took place to fulfill what the Lord had said through the prophet:
\N 这所有发生是要成全主藉着先知已说的：

% “Behold, the virgin will be with child and will give birth to a son, and they will call Him Immanuel” (which means, “God with us”f).
\N “看啊，童贞女会有孕并会生一个儿子，而他们会称祂为以马内利”（意思是：“上帝与我们同在”）。

% When Joseph woke up, he did as the angel of the Lord had commanded him and embraced Mary as his wife.
\N 当约瑟醒来，他按主的使者已命令他的做了并迎娶玛丽亚作了他的妻子。
% But he had no union with her until she gave birth to a Son. And he gave Him the name Jesus.
\N 但他与她没有结合，直到她生了一个儿子。而他将名字耶稣给了祂。

% Matthew 2
\section{第二章}

% The Pilgrimage of the Magi
\subsection*{贤士朝圣}

% After Jesus was born in Bethlehem in Judea, during the time of King Herod, Magi from the east arrived in Jerusalem,
\N 在耶稣出生在犹太的伯利恒之后，正值希律王时期，从东方来的贤士们抵达了耶路撒冷，
% asking, “Where is the One who has been born King of the Jews? We saw His star in the east and have come to worship Him.”
\N 问，“那一位已出生的犹太人的君王在哪里？我们在东方看见祂的星并来到这里敬拜祂。”

% When King Herod heard this, he was disturbed, and all Jerusalem with him.
\N 当希律王听说这，他心神不宁，而耶路撒冷所有人都与他一同感到不安。
% And when he had assembled all the chief priests and scribes of the people, he asked them where the Christ was to be born.
\N 而当他招聚了子民中所有的祭司长和律法师，他问他们基督要在哪里出生。

% “In Bethlehem in Judea,” they replied, “for this is what the prophet has written:
\N “在犹太的伯利恒，”他们回复，“因为这是先知已记的：

% ‘But you, Bethlehem, in the land of Judah, are by no means least among the rulers of Judah, for out of you will come a ruler who will be the shepherd of My people Israel.’”
\N ‘但是在犹大之地的伯利恒啊，你决不是犹大统治者之中最小的，因为一位统治者会从你而出，祂会是我以色列子民的牧者。’”

% Then Herod called the Magi secretly and learned from them the exact time the star had appeared.
\N 然后希律暗中叫来贤士们并从他们得知那星显现的精确时间。
% And sending them to Bethlehem, he said: “Go and search carefully for the Child, and when you find Him, report to me, so that I too may go and worship Him.”
\N 而在将他们派往伯利恒时，他说：“去仔细搜寻那婴孩，而当你们找到祂，就向我报告，以使我也能去敬拜祂。”

% After they had heard the king, they went on their way, and the star they had seen in the east went ahead of them until it stood over the place where the Child was.
\N 在他们听了那王的话之后，他们继续上路，而他们在东方看见的那颗星走在他们前头直到它定在那婴孩所在的位置上方。
% When they saw the star, they rejoiced with great delight.
\N 当他们看见那星，他们就以极大的喜悦欢欣。
% On coming to the house, they saw the Child with His mother Mary, and they fell down and worshiped Him. Then they opened their treasures and presented Him with gifts of gold and frankincense and myrrh.
\N 一进到那房子，他们看见那婴孩和祂母亲玛丽亚在一起，他们就俯倒并敬拜祂。然后他们打开他们的宝藏并将黄金、乳香和没药的礼物呈给祂。

% And having been warned in a dream not to return to Herod, they withdrew to their country by another route.
\N 因在梦中被警告不要返回到希律那里，他们就沿另一条路线撤到了他们的国家。

% The Flight to Egypt
\subsection*{逃到埃及}

% When the Magi had gone, an angel of the Lord appeared to Joseph in a dream. “Get up!” he said. “Take the Child and His mother and flee to Egypt. Stay there until I tell you, for Herod is going to search for the Child to kill Him.”
\N 当贤士们已走，主的一位使者在梦中向约瑟显现。“起身！”他说。“带上这婴孩和祂的母亲并逃到埃及。呆在那里直到我吩咐你，因为希律将要搜寻这婴孩以杀祂。”

% So he got up, took the Child and His mother by night, and withdrew to Egypt,
\N 所以他起身，连夜带上这婴孩和祂的母亲，并撤到了埃及，
% where he stayed until the death of Herod. This fulfilled what the Lord had spoken through the prophet: “Out of Egypt I called My Son.”
\N 他呆在那里直到希律去世。这成全了主藉着先知已讲的：“我召唤我的圣子出埃及。”

% Weeping and Great Mourning
\subsection*{哭泣与大哀}

% When Herod saw that he had been outwitted by the Magi, he was filled with rage. Sending orders, he put to death all the boys in Bethlehem and its vicinity who were two years old and under, according to the time he had learned from the Magi.
\N 当希律看见他已被贤士们愚弄，他就满腔怒火。他下达告令，按照他已从贤士们那里得知的时间，将在伯利恒与其邻地所有两岁及以内的男孩处死。
% Then what was spoken through the prophet Jeremiah was fulfilled:
\N 于是，藉着先知耶利米所讲的被成全：

% “A voice is heard in Ramah, weeping and great mourning, Rachel weeping for her children and refusing to be comforted, because they are no more.”
\N “在拉玛有个声音被听见，哭泣并大哀，拉结为她的子女哭泣并拒绝被安慰，因为他们不再有了。”

% The Return to Nazareth
\subsection*{返回拿撒勒}

% After Herod died, an angel of the Lord appeared in a dream to Joseph in Egypt.
\N 在希律死后，在埃及主的一位使者在梦中向约瑟显现。
% “Get up!” he said. “Take the Child and His mother and go to the land of Israel, for those seeking the Child’s life are now dead.”
\N “起身！”他说。“带上这婴孩和祂的母亲并去到以色列之地，因为那些索取这婴孩性命的人现在死了。”

% So Joseph got up, took the Child and His mother, and went to the land of Israel.
\N 所以约瑟起身，带上这婴孩和祂的母亲，并去到以色列之地。
% But when he learned that Archelaus was reigning in Judea in place of his father Herod, he was afraid to go there. And having been warned in a dream, he withdrew to the district of Galilee,
\N 但当他得知亚基老代替他父亲希律正在犹太为王时，他害怕去到那里。而在梦中被警告后，他撤到加利利的地域，
% and he went and lived in a town called Nazareth. So was fulfilled what was spoken through the prophets: “He will be called a Nazarene.”
\N 他就去生活在一个称为拿撒勒的小镇中。如此成就了藉着众先知已讲的：“祂会被称为拿撒勒人。”

% Matthew 3
\section{第三章}

% The Mission of John the Baptist
\subsection*{施洗约翰的使命}

% In those days John the Baptist came, preaching in the wilderness of Judea
\N 在那些日子，施洗约翰出来，在犹太的旷野传讲
% and saying, “Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is near.”
\N 并说，“要悔改，因为天国的国度近了。”
% This is he who was spoken of through the prophet Isaiah:
\N 这就是藉着先知以赛亚所讲到的那位：

% “A voice of one calling in the wilderness, ‘Prepare the way for the Lord, make straight paths for Him.’”
“一个人的声音在旷野中呼唤，‘为主预备道路，为祂修直路径。’”

% John wore a garment of camel’s hair, with a leather belt around his waist. His food was locusts and wild honey.
\N 约翰穿着骆驼毛的衣裳，在他腰间系着皮带。他的食物是蝗虫和野蜜。
% People went out to him from Jerusalem and all Judea and the whole region around the Jordan.
\N 族民从耶路撒冷、全犹太地和约旦四周整个地区出来到他那里。
% Confessing their sins, they were baptized by him in the Jordan River.
\N 忏悔他们的罪恶后，他们就在约旦河中受他的洗。

% But when John saw many of the Pharisees and Sadducees coming to his place of baptism, he said to them, “You brood of vipers, who warned you to flee from the coming wrath?
\N 但当约翰看见法利赛人和撒都该人中的许多来到他洗礼的地方，他对他们说，“你们这些毒蛇的孽种，谁警告你们来逃离即将到来的忿怒？
% Produce fruit, then, in keeping with repentance.
\N 要产出果实，以与悔改一致。
% And do not presume to say to yourselves, ‘We have Abraham as our father.’ For I tell you that out of these stones God can raise up children for Abraham.
\N 而不要妄自对自己说，‘我们有亚伯拉罕作我们的父亲，’因为我告诉你们出自这些石头上帝都能为亚伯拉罕兴起子女。
% The axe lies ready at the root of the trees, and every tree that does not produce good fruit will be cut down and thrown into the fire.
\N 斧头已备好放在树根上，而每一棵不产善果的树都会被砍倒并扔入火中。

% I baptize you with water for repentance, but after me will come One more powerful than I, whose sandals I am not worthy to carry. He will baptize you with the Holy Spirit and with fire.
\N 我用水为悔改为你们施洗，但在我之后会来比我更大能的一位，我连提他的鞋都不配。祂会用圣灵并用火为你们施洗。
% His winnowing fork is in His hand to clear His threshing floor and to gather His wheat into the barn; but He will burn up the chaff with unquenchable fire.”
\N 祂的扬谷叉在祂的手中要来清洁祂的禾场，并要将祂的麦子聚入谷仓；但祂会将糠秕用不灭之火烧尽。”

% The Baptism of Jesus
\subsection*{耶稣的洗礼}

% At that time Jesus came from Galilee to the Jordan to be baptized by John.
\N 在那时耶稣从加利利来到约旦要受约翰的洗。
% But John tried to prevent Him, saying, “I need to be baptized by You, and do You come to me?”
\N 但约翰试图阻止祂，说，“我需要受祢的洗，祢却到我这里吗？”

% “Let it be so now,” Jesus replied. “It is fitting for us to fulfill all righteousness in this way.” Then John permitted Him.
\N “现在要让它如此，”耶稣回复。“我们以这方式成就所有的正义是适合的。”然后约翰准许了祂。

% As soon as Jesus was baptized, He went up out of the water. Suddenly the heavens were opened, and He saw the Spirit of God descending like a dove and resting on Him.
\N 耶稣一受洗，祂就从水里上来。一瞬之间诸天被打开了，而祂看见上帝的圣灵像鸽子一样降落，并歇在了祂身上。
% And a voice from heaven said, “This is My beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased!”
\N 而一个话音从天国而来说：“这是我所爱的圣子，我对祂非常满意。”

% Matthew 4
\section{第四章}

% The Temptation of Jesus
\subsection*{耶稣受诱惑}

% Then Jesus was led by the Spirit into the wilderness to be tempted by the devil.
\N 然后耶稣被圣灵引导进入旷野来被魔鬼诱惑。
% After fasting forty days and forty nights, He was hungry.
\N 在禁食四十天四十夜之后，祂饿了。

% The tempter came to Him and said, “If You are the Son of God, tell these stones to become bread.”
\N 那诱惑者到祂那里并说，“如果祢是上帝的圣子，就吩咐这些石头变成饼。”

% But Jesus answered, “It is written:
\N 但耶稣回答，“经上记着：

% ‘Man shall not live on bread alone, but on every word that comes from the mouth of God.’”
‘人不应惟独靠饼存活，而靠来自上帝口中的每一句词。’”

% Then the devil took Him to the holy city and set Him on the pinnacle of the temple.
\N 然后魔鬼将祂带到圣城并将祂放置在神殿的塔尖上。
% “If You are the Son of God,” he said, “throw Yourself down. For it is written:
\N “如果祢是上帝的圣子，”他说，“将祢自己扔下去。因为经上记着：

% ‘He will command His angels concerning You, and they will lift You up in their hands, so that You will not strike Your foot against a stone.’”
‘祂会命令祂的众使者关照祢，而他们会用他们的手将祢举起，以使祢不会让祢的脚踢到一块石头。’”

% Jesus replied, “It is also written: ‘Do not put the Lord your God to the test.’”
\N 耶稣回复，“经上也记着：‘不要将主你的上帝放在试探中。’”

% Again, the devil took Him to a very high mountain and showed Him all the kingdoms of the world and their glory.
\N 再次，魔鬼将祂带到一座非常高的山峰上并向祂展示世界所有的国度以及他们的荣耀。
% “All this I will give You,” he said, “if You will fall down and worship me.”
\N “所有这我都会给祢，”他说，“只要祢会俯倒并敬拜我。”

% “Away from Me, Satan!” Jesus told him. “For it is written: ‘Worship the Lord your God and serve Him only.’”
\N “撒旦，远离我!”耶稣吩咐他。“因为经上记着：‘要敬拜主你的上帝，并惟独侍奉祂。’”

% Then the devil left Him, and angels came and ministered to Him.
\N 然后魔鬼离开了祂，而众天使就过来侍候祂。

% Jesus Begins His Ministry
\subsection*{耶稣开始祂的事工}

% When Jesus heard that John had been imprisoned, He withdrew to Galilee.
\N 当耶稣听说约翰已被囚禁，祂撤到了加利利。
% Leaving Nazareth, He went and lived in Capernaum, which is by the sea in the region of Zebulun and Naphtali,
\N 离开拿撒勒，祂去生活在迦百农，那里靠海在西布伦和拿弗他利地区，
% to fulfill what was spoken through the prophet Isaiah:
\N 为成就藉着先知以赛亚所讲的：

% “Land of Zebulun and land of Naphtali, the Way of the Sea, beyond the Jordan, Galilee of the Gentiles—
\N “西布伦之地和拿弗他利之地，海洋之路，超出约旦，外邦人的加利利---
% the people living in darkness have seen a great light; on those living in the land of the shadow of death,a light has dawned.”
\N 活在黑暗中的子民已看见一个巨大的光体；在那些生活在死荫之地的人之上一个光体已破晓。”

% From that time on Jesus began to preach, “Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is near.”
\N 从那时起耶稣开始传讲，“要悔改，因为天国的国度近了。”

% The First Disciples
\subsection*{最早的门徒}

% As Jesus was walking beside the Sea of Galilee, He saw two brothers, Simon called Peter and his brother Andrew. They were casting a net into the sea, for they were fishermen.
\N 当耶稣在加利利海边行走时，祂看见两兄弟：被称为彼得的西门和他的兄弟安得烈。他们正在将网撒入海中，因为他们是渔夫。
% “Come, follow Me,” Jesus said, “and I will make you fishers of men.”
\N “来，跟随我，”耶稣说，“而我会使你们成为得人的渔夫。”
% And at once they left their nets and followed Him.
\N 而他们立即丢下他们的网并跟从了祂。

% Going on from there, He saw two other brothers, James son of Zebedee and his brother John. They were in a boat with their father Zebedee, mending their nets. Jesus called them,
\N 从那里继续走，祂看见另外两兄弟：西庇太的儿子雅各和他兄弟约翰。他们与他们的父亲西庇太一起在船上修补他们的网。耶稣召唤他们，
% and immediately they left the boat and their father and followed Him.
\N 而他们立刻离开船和他们的父亲并跟从了祂。

% Jesus Heals the Multitudes
\subsection*{耶稣治愈众多人}

% Jesus went throughout Galilee, teaching in their synagogues, preaching the gospel of the kingdom, and healing every disease and sickness among the people.
\N 耶稣走遍加利利，在他们的会堂中教导，传讲国度的福音，并治愈子民中间每种疾病和病痛。
% News about Him spread all over Syria, and people brought to Him all who were ill with various diseases, those suffering acute pain, the demon-possessed, those having seizures, and the paralyzed, and He healed them.
\N 关于祂的讯息扩散至全叙利亚，而族民将所有患各种疾病的人带给祂，那些遭受急性疼痛的，被邪灵附身的，那些有癫痫的，以及那些瘫痪的，而祂治愈了他们。

% Large crowds followed Him, having come from Galilee, the Decapolis, Jerusalem, Judea, and beyond the Jordan.
\N 大量的人群从加利利，低加坡里，耶路撒冷，犹太，以及约旦之外而来跟从祂。

% Matthew 5
\section{第五章}

% The Sermon on the Mount
\subsection*{山上宝训}

% When Jesus saw the crowds, He went up on the mountain and sat down. His disciples came to Him,
\N 当耶稣看见人群，祂登上山并坐下。祂的门徒们到了祂那里，
% and He began to teach them, saying:
\N 而祂开始教导他们，说：

% The Beatitudes
\subsubsection*{八福}

% “Blessed are the poor in spirit, for theirs is the kingdom of heaven.
\N “灵里贫穷的人是有福的，因为天国的国度是他们的。

% Blessed are those who mourn, for they will be comforted.
\N 那些哀悼的人是有福的，因为他们会被安慰。

% Blessed are the meek, for they will inherit the earth.
\N 温顺的人是有福的，因为他们会继承大地。
    \footnote{ 或：那些克制发力的人有福了，因为他们将继承地土。 }

% Blessed are those who hunger and thirst for righteousness, for they will be filled.
\N 那些饥渴慕义的人是有福的，因为他们会被充满。

% Blessed are the merciful, for they will be shown mercy.
\N 宽容的人是有福的，因为他们会被展示宽容。

% Blessed are the pure in heart, for they will see God.
\N 心中纯洁的人是有福的，因为他们会看见上帝。

% Blessed are the peacemakers, for they will be called sons of God.
\N 缔造和平的人是有福的，因为他们会被称为上帝的子嗣。

% Blessed are those who are persecuted because of righteousness, for theirs is the kingdom of heaven.
\N 那些因为正义被迫害的人是有福的，因为天国的国度是他们的。\\
% Blessed are you when people insult you, persecute you, and falsely say all kinds of evil against you because of Me.
\N 当民众因为我而侮辱你们，迫害你们，并妄说各种恶事针对你们时，你们是有福的。
% Rejoice and be glad, because great is your reward in heaven, for in the same way they persecuted the prophets before you.
\N 要欢欣并高兴，因为你们在天国的赏赐是巨大的，因为他们用相同的方式迫害在你们之前的众先知。

% Salt and Light
\subsubsection*{盐与光}

% You are the salt of the earth. But if the salt loses its savor, how can it be made salty again? It is no longer good for anything, except to be thrown out and trampled by men.
\N 你们是地上的盐。但如果盐失去它的滋味，怎么能再次被变咸呢？它不再对任何东西有益，除了被扔出去被人践踏。

% You are the light of the world. A city on a hill cannot be hidden.
\N 你们是这世界的光。一座在山上的城是无法被隐藏的。
% Neither do people light a lamp and put it under a basket. Instead, they set it on a stand, and it gives light to everyone in the house.
\N 民众也不会点亮一盏灯而将其放在斗底下。取而代之，他们将它放在灯台上，而它将光给房子中的每个人。
% In the same way, let your light shine before men, that they may see your good deeds and glorify your Father in heaven.
\N 以同样的方式，让你们的光在人们面前照耀，以使他们能看见你们的善迹并荣耀你们在天国的圣父。

% The Fulfillment of the Law
\subsubsection*{律法的成全}

% Do not think that I have come to abolish the Law or the Prophets. I have not come to abolish them, but to fulfill them.
\N 不要以为我已来是要废除律法或先知书；我未曾为废除它们而来，而是为成全它们。
% For truly I tell you, until heaven and earth disappear, not the smallest letter, not the least stroke of a pen, will by any means disappear from the Law until everything is accomplished.
\N 因为我实在告诉你们，直到天与地消失，连最小的字母，和一笔最少的笔画，都不会以任何手段从律法中消失，直至一切都被成就。
% Therefore, anyone who sets aside one of the least of these commandments and teaches others accordingly will be called least in the kingdom of heaven, but whoever practices and teaches these commands will be called great in the kingdom of heaven.
\N 因此，任何人搁置这些诫命中最小的一条，并相应地教导其他人的，将在天国的国度中被称为最小的，但无论谁实践并教导这些命令，将在天国的国度中被称伟大的。
% For I tell you that unless your righteousness surpasses that of the Pharisees and the teachers of the law, you will certainly not enter the kingdom of heaven.
\N 因为我告诉你们，除非你们的义超越那法利赛人和律法师的，否则你们肯定不会进入天国的国度。

% Anger and Reconciliation
\subsubsection*{愤怒与和解}

% You have heard that it was said to the ancients, ‘Do not murder’ and ‘Anyone who murders will be subject to judgment.’
\N 你们听过经上对古人说，‘不要谋杀’以及‘任何谋杀人的都会遭受审判的制裁。’
% But I tell you that anyone who is angry with his brother will be subject to judgment. Again, anyone who says to his brother, ‘Raca,’ will be subject to the Sanhedrin. But anyone who says, ‘You fool!’ will be subject to the fire of hell.
\N 但我告诉你们，任何对他兄弟愤怒
    \footnote{ 有的版本包含：无缘故。 }
的人都会遭受审判的制裁。再者，任何对他的兄弟说‘拉加’
    \footnote{ 拉加在亚兰文中是表达轻蔑的意思。 }
的人都会遭受议会的制裁。但任何说‘你这蠢货！’的人都会遭受地狱之火的制裁。

% So if you are offering your gift at the altar and there remember that your brother has something against you, 
\N 所以如果你正在祭坛上奉上你的礼物，而在那里记起你的兄弟有一些事情针对你，
% leave your gift there before the altar. First go and be reconciled to your brother; then come and offer your gift.
\N 要在祭坛前将你的礼物留在那里。首先去与你的兄弟和解；然后来奉上你的礼物。

% Reconcile quickly with your adversary, while you are still on the way to court. Otherwise, he may hand you over to the judge, and the judge may hand you over to the officer, and you may be thrown into prison.
\N 在你还在上法庭的路上时，要迅速与你的对手和解。否则，他可能会把你交送法官，而法官可能会把你交给官员，你可能就被投进监狱。
% Truly I tell you, you will not get out until you have paid the last penny.
\N 我实在告诉你们，你不会出来直到你付完最后一分钱。

% Adultery
\subsubsection*{通奸}

% You have heard that it was said, ‘Do not commit adultery.’
\N 你们听过经上说，‘不要犯通奸。’ 
% But I tell you that anyone who looks at a woman to lust after her has already committed adultery with her in his heart.
\N 但我告诉你们，任何人看到一个女人而欲念她，就已在他心中与她犯了通奸。
% If your right eye causes you to sin, gouge it out and throw it away. It is better for you to lose one part of your body than for your whole body to be thrown into hell.
\N 如果你的右眼致使你犯罪，就将它剜出来并扔掉。你失去你身体的一部分总比你的整个身体被扔入地狱要好。
% And if your right hand causes you to sin, cut it off and throw it away. It is better for you to lose one part of your body than for your whole body to depart into hell.
\N 如果你的右手致使你犯罪，就将它砍断并扔掉。你失去你身体的一部分总比你的整个身体离开进入地狱要好。

% Divorce
\subsubsection*{离婚}

% It has also been said, ‘Whoever divorces his wife must give her a certificate of divorce.’
\N 经上也说过，‘任何与妻子离婚的人，必须给她一纸休书。’
% But I tell you that anyone who divorces his wife, except for sexual immorality, brings adultery upon her. And he who marries a divorced woman commits adultery.
\N 但我告诉你们，任何与妻子离婚的人，除非因为淫乱，都招致她通奸。而娶了一个离婚女人的人也犯了通奸。

% Oaths and Vows
\subsubsection*{誓言与誓约}

% Again, you have heard that it was said to the ancients, ‘Do not break your oath, but fulfill your vows to the Lord.’
\N 再次，你们听过经上对古人说，‘不要破坏你的誓言，而要成全你对主的誓约。’
% But I tell you not to swear at all: either by heaven, for it is God’s throne;
\N 但我告诉你们，根本不要发誓：无论是以天发誓，因为它是上帝的宝座；
% or by the earth, for it is His footstool; or by Jerusalem, for it is the city of the Great King.
\N 或以地发誓，因为它是祂的脚凳；或以耶路撒冷发誓，因为它是大君王的城。
% And do not swear by your head, for you cannot make even one hair white or black.
\N 也不要以你的头发誓，因为你甚至不能使一根头发变白或变黑。
% All you need to say is simply ‘Yes’ or ‘No’; anything beyond this comes from the evil one.
\N 所有你们需要说的就简单是‘是’或‘否’；超过这的任何都来自恶魔。

% Love Your Enemies
\subsubsection*{爱你们的敌人}

% You have heard that it was said, ‘Eye for eye and tooth for tooth.’
\N 你们听过经上说，‘以眼还眼，以牙还牙。’
% But I tell you not to resist an evil person. If someone slaps you on your right cheek, turn to him the other also;
\N 但我告诉你们，不要反抗一个邪恶的个人。如果有人扇你打在你的右脸上，要将另一边也转向他；
% if someone wants to sue you and take your tunic, let him have your cloak as well;
\N 如果有人想要控诉你并拿走你的内袍，让他也拿走你的披风；
% and if someone forces you to go one mile, go with him two miles.
\N 而如果有人强迫你走一里，就和他一起走两里。
% Give to the one who asks you, and do not turn away from the one who wants to borrow from you.
\N 要施予那问求你的人，并不要转离那想要向你借取的人。

% You have heard that it was said, ‘Love your neighbor’ and ‘Hate your enemy.’
\N 你们听过经上说，‘爱你的邻居’和‘恨你的敌人。’
% But I tell you, love your enemies and pray for those who persecute you,
\N 但我告诉你们，要爱你们的敌人并为那些迫害你们的人祷告，
    \footnote{ 有的版本：爱你们的敌人，祝福那些咒诅你们的人，向那些恨你们的人行善，并为那些迫害你们的人祷告，}
% that you may be sons of your Father in heaven. He causes His sun to rise on the evil and the good, and sends rain on the righteous and the unrighteous.
\N 以使你们能成为你们在天国的圣父的子嗣。祂使祂的太阳在恶人和好人上升起，并降雨在义人和不义之人上。
% If you love those who love you, what reward will you get? Do not even tax collectors do the same?
\N 如果你们爱那些爱你们的人，你们会得到什么赏赐呢？甚至税吏不也一样这么做吗？
% And if you greet only your brothers, what are you doing more than others? Do not even pagans do that?
\N 而如果你们只问候你们的兄弟们，什么是你们做的比别人更多呢？甚至异教徒不也做那吗？

% Be perfect, therefore, as your heavenly Father is perfect.
\N 所以，要臻备，如你们天国的圣父是完美的一样。

% chapter 6
\section{第六章}

% Giving to the Needy
\subsubsection*{施予贫困者}

% “Be careful not to perform your righteous acts before men to be seen by them. If you do, you will have no reward from your Father in heaven.
\N “要谨慎，不要在人们面前实行你们的义行来被他们看见。如果你们这样做，你们就不会有来自你们在天国的圣父的赏赐。

% So when you give to the needy, do not sound a trumpet before you, as the hypocrites do in the synagogues and on the streets, to be honored by men. Truly I tell you, they already have their full reward.
\N 所以当你施予贫困者时，不要在你前面吹响号角，像伪善者在会堂和街道上所做的，来被人们尊敬。我实在告诉你们，他们已经有了他们全额的赏赐。
% But when you give to the needy, do not let your left hand know what your right hand is doing,
\N 但当你施予贫困者时，不要让你的左手知道你的右手在做什么，
% so that your giving may be in secret. Then your Father, who sees what is done in secret, will reward you.
\N 以使你的施予能在暗中。然后你的圣父，祂看见在暗中所做的，就会赏赐你。

% The Lord’s Prayer
\subsubsection*{主祷文}

% And when you pray, do not be like the hypocrites. For they love to pray standing in the synagogues and on the street corners to be seen by men. Truly I tell you, they already have their full reward.
\N 而当你们祷告时，不要像伪善者一样。因为他们爱站在会堂中和街角上祷告以被人们看见。我实在告诉你们，他们已经有了他们全额的赏赐。
% But when you pray, go into your inner room, shut your door, and pray to your Father, who is unseen. And your Father, who sees what is done in secret, will reward you.
\N 当你们祷告时，而是要走进你的里屋，关上你的门，并向你的圣父祷告，祂是看不见的。而你的圣父，祂看见在暗中所完成的，就会赏赐你。

% And when you pray, do not babble on like pagans, for they think that by their many words they will be heard.
\N 而当你们祷告时，不要像异教徒一样唠叨不停，因为他们以为靠他们许多言词，他们就会被听见。
% Do not be like them, for your Father knows what you need before you ask Him.
\N 不要像他们一样，因为你的圣父在你问求祂之前就知道你需要什么。

% So then, this is how you should pray:
\N 所以，这是你们应该祷告的方式：

% ‘Our Father in heaven, hallowed be Your name.
‘我们在天国中的圣父，愿祢的名被尊为圣。

% Your kingdom come, Your will be done, on earth as it is in heaven.
\N 愿祢的国度到来，愿祢的意志被完成，在地上如在天国中一样。

% Give us this day our daily bread.
\N 我们日用的食粮今日赐给我们。

% And forgive us our debts, as we also have forgiven our debtors.
\N 并对我们饶恕我们的债，如我们也已饶恕我们的欠债人一样。

% And lead us not into temptation, but deliver us from the evil one. For Yours is the kingdom and the power and the glory, forever. Amen.’
\N 而且带领我们不进入诱惑，而要解救我们脱离恶魔。因为国度、大能和荣耀永远是祢的。阿们。’

% For if you forgive men their trespasses, your heavenly Father will also forgive you.
\N 因为如果你们对人们饶恕他们的过犯，你们天国的圣父也会饶恕你们。
% But if you do not forgive men their trespasses, neither will your Father forgive yours.
\N 但如果你们不对人们饶恕他们的过犯，你们的圣父也不会饶恕你们的过犯。

% Proper Fasting
\subsubsection*{恰当的禁食}

% When you fast, do not be somber like the hypocrites, for they disfigure their faces to show men they are fasting. Truly I tell you, they already have their full reward.
\N 当你们禁食时，不要像伪善者一样忧郁，因为他们使他们的脸难看以向其他人展示他们在禁食。我实在告诉你们，他们已经有了他们全额的赏赐。
% But when you fast, anoint your head and wash your face,
\N 但当你们禁食时，要膏你的头并洗你的脸，
% so that your fasting will not be obvious to men, but only to your Father, who is unseen. And your Father, who sees what is done in secret, will reward you.
\N 以使你的禁食对人们不会是明显的，但惟对你的圣父是明显的，祂是看不见的。而你的圣父，祂看见在暗中所完成的，就会赏赐你。

% Treasures in Heaven
\subsubsection*{天国中的宝藏}

% Do not store up for yourselves treasures on earth, where moth and rust destroy, and where thieves break in and steal.
\N 不要为你们自己在地上囤积宝藏，那里有蛀虫和锈蚀来摧毁，还有窃贼闯入并偷窃。
% But store up for yourselves treasures in heaven, where moth and rust do not destroy, and where thieves do not break in and steal.
\N 而要为你们自己在天国囤积宝藏，那里蛀虫和锈蚀摧毁不了，窃贼也不能闯入并偷窃。
% For where your treasure is, there your heart will be also.
\N 因为你的宝藏在哪里，你的心也会在哪里。

% The Lamp of the Body
\subsubsection*{身体的灯}

% The eye is the lamp of the body. If your eyes are good, your whole body will be full of light. 
\N 眼睛是身体的灯。如果你的眼睛是好的，你的整个身体会充满光明。
% But if your eyes are bad, your whole body will be full of darkness. If then the light within you is darkness, how great is that darkness!
\N 但如果你的眼睛是坏的，你的整个身体就会充满黑暗。如果你内在的光是黑暗，那么那黑暗是多么巨大！

% No one can serve two masters: Either he will hate the one and love the other, or he will be devoted to the one and despise the other. You cannot serve both God and money.
\N 无人能侍奉两个主人：或者他会恨这个而爱那个，或者他会献身给这个而鄙视那个。你不能既侍奉上帝又侍奉金钱。

% Do Not Worry
\subsubsection*{不要忧虑}

% Therefore I tell you, do not worry about your life, what you will eat or drink; or about your body, what you will wear. Is not life more than food, and the body more than clothes?
\N 因此我告诉你们，不要为你的生命忧虑你会吃或喝什么；或是为你的身体忧虑你会穿什么。生命难道不大过食物，而身体不大过衣服吗？
% Look at the birds of the air: They do not sow or reap or gather into barns, and yet your heavenly Father feeds them. Are you not much more valuable than they?
\N 看那空中的鸟：它们既不播种也不收割也不聚入谷仓，而你天国的圣父仍然喂养它们。你们难道不比它们更有价值吗？
% Who of you by worrying can add a single hour to his life?
\N 你们谁能靠忧虑为他的生命添加那怕一个时辰呢？

% And why do you worry about clothes? Consider how the lilies of the field grow: They do not labor or spin.
\N 而你们为何为衣服忧虑呢？思虑田野的百合花如何生长：它们既不操劳也不纺织。
% Yet I tell you that not even Solomon in all his glory was adorned like one of these.
\N 但我告诉你们甚至所罗门在他所有荣耀中都没有像这些中的一个一样被装饰。
% If that is how God clothes the grass of the field, which is here today and tomorrow is thrown into the furnace, will He not much more clothe you, O you of little faith?
\N 如果那是上帝如何给田野的草穿戴的，它今天在这里而明天被扔进火炉中。你们这些小信的人啊，祂难道不会给给你们穿戴更多？

% Therefore do not worry, saying, ‘What shall we eat?’ or ‘What shall we drink?’ or ‘What shall we wear?’
\N 因此不要忧虑，说，‘我们该吃什么？’或‘我们该喝什么？’或‘我们该穿什么？’
% For the Gentiles strive after all these things, and your heavenly Father knows that you need them.
\N 因为外邦人为所有这些事情奋斗，而你们天国的圣父知道你们需要它们。
% But seek first the kingdom of God and His righteousness, and all these things will be added unto you.
\N 但首先寻求上帝的国度和祂的正义，而所有这些东西都会被添加给你们。

% Therefore do not worry about tomorrow, for tomorrow will worry about itself. Today has enough trouble of its own.
\N 因此不要为明天忧虑，因为明天会为它自己忧虑。今天有它自身够多的烦恼了。

% chapter 7
\section{第七章}

% Judging Others
\subsubsection*{评判他人}

% “Do not judge, or you will be judged.
\N “不要评判，否则你们也会被评判。
% For with the same judgment you pronounce, you will be judged, and with the measure you use, it will be measured to you.
\N 因为你们会以你们宣判的相同的判断来被评判，而你们使用的尺度，会被用来测量你们。

% Why do you look at the speck in your brother’s eye but fail to notice the beam in your own eye?
\N 为何你们看见那在你兄弟眼中的木屑而未能注意你自身眼中的梁木呢？
% How can you say to your brother, ‘Let me take the speck out of your eye,’ while there is still a beam in your own eye?
\N 你怎么能对你的兄弟说，‘让我将木屑从你眼里取出，’而在你自身眼里依然还有梁木呢？
% You hypocrite! First take the beam out of your own eye, and then you will see clearly to remove the speck from your brother’s eye.
\N 你这伪善者！首先从你自身眼里取出梁木，然后你会清晰地看见以从你兄弟眼中移除木屑。

% Do not give dogs what is holy; do not throw your pearls before swine. If you do, they may trample them under their feet, and then turn and tear you to pieces.
\N 不要将圣物给狗；不要将你们的珍珠扔在猪面前。如果你们这样做，他们可能将它们践踏在他们脚下，然后转身并将你们撕成碎片。

% Ask, Seek, Knock
\subsubsection*{问求、寻求、敲门}

% Ask, and it will be given to you; seek, and you will find; knock, and the door will be opened to you.
\N 问求，它就会被赐给你；寻求，你就会找到；敲门，门就会向你被敞开。
% For everyone who asks receives; he who seeks finds; and to him who knocks, the door will be opened.
\N 因为每一个寻求的都领受；寻求的人就找到；而对敲门的人，门就会被敞开。

% Which of you, if his son asks for bread, will give him a stone?
\N 你们有谁，如果他的儿子要饼，会给他石头呢？
% Or if he asks for a fish, will give him a snake?
\N 或者如果他要鱼，会给他蛇呢？
% So if you who are evil know how to give good gifts to your children, how much more will your Father in heaven give good things to those who ask Him!
\N 所以如果你们这些恶人知道如何给你们的儿女好的礼物，你们在天国的圣父岂不会更加将善物赐给那些问求祂的人！

% In everything, then, do to others as you would have them do to you. For this is the essence of the Law and the Prophets.
\N 于是，在一切事情上，要对他人做你想要他们对你做的事情。因为这是律法和先知书的精髓。

% The Narrow Gate
\subsubsection*{窄门}

% Enter through the narrow gate. For wide is the gate and broad is the way that leads to destruction, and many enter through it.
\N 要从窄门进入。因为导向毁灭的门是宽的，路是阔的，并且许多人从它进入。
% But small is the gate and narrow the way that leads to life, and only a few find it.
\N 但导向生命的门是小的，路是窄的，并且只有少数人找到它。

% A Tree and Its Fruit
\subsubsection*{树和它的果实}

% Beware of false prophets. They come to you in sheep’s clothing, but inwardly they are ravenous wolves.
\N 要提防假先知。他们披着羊衣来到你们这里，但他们里面是贪掠的狼。
% By their fruit you will recognize them. Are grapes gathered from thornbushes, or figs from thistles?
\N 你们由他们的果实就会认出他们。葡萄是从荆棘丛采集的，或者无花果来自刺蓟吗？
% Likewise, every good tree bears good fruit, but a bad tree bears bad fruit.
\N 类似地，每棵好树都结善果，但一棵坏树就结恶果。
% A good tree cannot bear bad fruit, and a bad tree cannot bear good fruit.
\N 一棵好树不能结恶果，而一棵坏树不能结善果。
% Every tree that does not bear good fruit is cut down and thrown into the fire.
\N 每棵不结善果的树都被砍倒并扔入火中。
% So then, by their fruit you will recognize them.
\N 所以，你们由他们的果实会认出他们。

% Not everyone who says to Me, ‘Lord, Lord,’ will enter the kingdom of heaven, but only he who does the will of My Father in heaven.
\N 不是每个对我说，‘主啊，主啊’的人都会进入天国的国度，而惟有行我天国中圣父意志的人。
% Many will say to Me on that day, ‘Lord, Lord, did we not prophesy in Your name, and in Your name drive out demons and perform many miracles?’
\N 许多人会在那天对我说：‘主啊，主啊，我们难道不是奉祢的名说神启、奉祢的名驱除邪灵，并实行许多奇迹吗？’

% Then I will tell them plainly, ‘I never knew you; depart from Me, you workers of lawlessness!’
\N 那么我会直白地告诉他们，‘我从不认识你们；离开我，你们这些不法的践行者！’

% The House on the Rock
\subsubsection*{岩石上的房子}

% Therefore everyone who hears these words of Mine and acts on them is like a wise man who built his house on the rock.
\N 因此，每个听见我这些言词并按它们行动的人就像一个智者，将他房子建造在岩石上。
% The rain fell, the torrents raged, and the winds blew and beat against that house; yet it did not fall, because its foundation was on the rock.
\N 雨降下，洪流狂暴，而风吹并击打那房子；它仍然不倒，因为它的根基是在岩石上。

% But everyone who hears these words of Mine and does not act on them is like a foolish man who built his house on sand.
\N 但每个听见我这些言词而不按它们行动的就像一个蠢人，将他的房子建造在沙上。
% The rain fell, the torrents raged, and the winds blew and beat against that house, and it fell—and great was its collapse!”
\N 雨降下，洪流狂暴，而风吹并击打那房子；它就倒了---并且它的倒塌很大！”

% The Authority of Jesus
\subsection*{耶稣的权柄}

% When Jesus had finished saying these things, the crowds were astonished at His teaching,
\N 当耶稣结束完说这些事情时，人群对祂的教导感到震惊，
% because He taught as one who had authority, and not as their scribes.
\N 因为祂教导得像一个有权柄的人，而不像他们的律法师。

% chapter 8
\section{第八章}

% The Leper’s Prayer
\subsection*{麻风病人的祷告}

% When Jesus came down from the mountain, large crowds followed Him.
\N 当耶稣从山上下来，大量的人群跟随祂。
% Suddenly a leper came and knelt before Him, saying, “Lord, if You are willing, You can make me clean.”
\N 突然一个麻风病人来到并跪在祂面前，说，“主啊，如果祢愿意，祢能使我洁净。”

% Jesus reached out His hand and touched the man. “I am willing,” He said. “Be clean!” And immediately his leprosy was cleansed.
\N 耶稣伸出祂的手并触摸那人。“我愿意，”祂说。“洁净吧！”立刻他的麻风病就被洁净了。

% Then Jesus instructed him, “See that you don’t tell anyone. But go, show yourself to the priest and offer the gift prescribed by Moses, as a testimony to them.”
\N 然后耶稣指示他，“当心！千万不要告诉任何人。但去吧，将你自己给祭司验视并奉上摩西所规定的祭物，作为对他们的凭证。”

% The Faith of the Centurion
\subsection*{百夫长的信心}

% When Jesus had entered Capernaum, a centurion came and pleaded with Him,
\N 耶稣进入迦百农之后，一个百夫长前来恳求祂，
% “Lord, my servant lies at home, paralyzed and in terrible agony.”
\N “主啊，我的仆人卧倒在屋里，瘫痪了，正遭受可怕的折磨。”

% “I will go and heal him,” Jesus replied.
\N “我亲自去医治他，”耶稣回复。

% The centurion answered, “Lord, I am not worthy to have You come under my roof. But just say the word, and my servant will be healed.
\N 百夫长回答，“主啊，我不配让祢到我屋檐下。但只说一句话，我的仆人就会被治愈。
% For I myself am a man under authority, with soldiers under me. I tell one to go, and he goes, and another to come, and he comes. I tell my servant to do something, and he does it.”
\N 因为我自己是个被派在权柄下的人，麾下有兵。我叫一个“去”，他就去；叫另一个“来”，他就来。我叫我的仆人“做这”，他就去做。”

% When Jesus heard this, He marveled and said to those following Him, “Truly I tell you, I have not found anyone in Israel with such great faith.
\N 耶稣听了就惊叹，并对跟随祂的人说：“我实在告诉你们，我在以色列未曾在任何人那里找着如此大的信心。”
